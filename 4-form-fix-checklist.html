Form Fix Checklist

Master the big 3 lifts with these essential form cues

🏋️ Back Squat

Bar Position
Bar resting on rear delts, not on neck. Hands outside shoulders, tight grip pulling bar into your back.
Foot Placement
Shoulder width stance, toes slightly out (10 to 15°). Weight distributed evenly across full foot.
Brace & Descent
Big breath into belly, brace abs like you're about to be punched. Hips back first, then knees bend. Maintain neutral spine.
Depth & Position
Hip crease below top of knee. Knees tracking over toes, not caving in. Chest up, eyes neutral.
The Drive
Push floor away, knees and hips extend together. Maintain torso angle until lockout. Don't shoot hips up first.
Mental Cue
"Spread the floor apart with your feet" — activates glutes and prevents knee cave

💪 Bench Press

Setup Position
Shoulder blades squeezed together and down. Natural arch in lower back. Glutes on bench, feet flat and stable.
Grip Width
Hands slightly wider than shoulder width. Wrists stacked above elbows at bottom. Bar across base of palm, not fingers.
Bar Path
Touch point at mid chest to nipple line. Slight arc from rack to chest. Elbows at 45° angle, not flared to 90°.
Leg Drive
Push feet into floor throughout lift. Creates whole body tension. Prevents shoulder lifting off bench.
The Press
Controlled descent, brief pause on chest. Explode up, driving bar back toward rack position. Lock out fully.
Mental Cue
"Bend the bar" — engages lats and creates stable pressing platform

⚡ Deadlift

Starting Position
Bar over mid foot. Shins vertical or nearly vertical. Shoulders directly above or slightly in front of bar.
Grip & Arms
Double overhand or mixed grip just outside legs. Arms straight, hanging like ropes. Pull slack out of bar before lift.
Spinal Position
Neutral spine from head to hips. Chest up, lats engaged (imagine squeezing oranges in armpits). No rounding.
The Pull
Big breath and brace. Push floor away with legs. Bar stays in contact with legs. Hips and shoulders rise together.
Lockout
Stand tall, hips fully extended. Don't hyperextend back. Controlled descent, same path down as up.
Mental Cue
"Leg press the floor" — shifts focus to legs driving, not back pulling
