The Truth About My 12 Weeks

Last year I decided to do a proper photoshoot. Started at 76kg, ended at 63kg in 12 weeks.

I trained 4 to 5 times a week, built cardio up to 220 minutes by the end, and ran separate training day and rest day calories with strategic refeed days.

The hunger wasn't the hard part. The mental battle of staying consistent when progress slowed was the real fight.

Here's The Exact Strategy I Used

Week by week calories, macros, cardio prescription, and what actually happened in each phase.

Weeks 1-4: Foundation
76kg → 72kg (-4kg)
2200 Training Days
1800 Rest Days
152g Protein
60g Fat
Cardio 2x per week, 20min each
Training 4x per week, full body
Refeed None yet
Lost 4kg in first month, mostly water and some fat. Hunger was manageable. Energy stayed high. This phase felt easy because I hadn't been dieting long.
Weeks 5-8: The Grind
72kg → 68kg (-4kg)
2000 Training Days
1700 Rest Days
152g Protein
55g Fat
Cardio 3x per week, 30min each
Training 4x per week, upper/lower
Refeed Every 10 days (2500 cals)
Progress slowed down. This is where most people panic and slash calories harder. I stayed disciplined, kept protein high, and added a refeed every 10 days. Lost another 4kg but had to fight for it mentally.
Weeks 9-12: The Finish
68kg → 63kg (-5kg)
1900 Training Days
1600 Rest Days
152g Protein
50g Fat
Cardio 4-5x per week, 40-50min
Training 4x per week, push/pull/legs
Refeed Every 7 days (2600 cals)
Hardest phase by far. I was lean, tired, and hungry most days. But I was close to the finish line. Increased refeed frequency to keep metabolism going. Final 5kg came off. Worth every single minute of discomfort.
