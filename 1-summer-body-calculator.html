The Truth About My 12 Weeks

Last year I decided to do a proper photoshoot. Started at 76kg, ended at 63kg in 12 weeks.

I trained 4 to 5 times a week, incremented cardio up to 220 minutes by the end, and ran training day and non-training day calories with refeed days here and there.

Everyone asks if I was starving the whole time. Honestly? The hunger wasn't the hard part. The mental battle was.

Here's Exactly How I Did It

Week by week breakdown of calories, cardio, and what actually happened. This is the blueprint.

Weeks 1-4: Foundation
76kg → 72kg
Training Days 2200 calories
Rest Days 1800 calories
Cardio 2x per week, 20min
Protein 152g daily (2g/kg)
Lost 4kg in first month. Mostly water and some fat. Hunger was manageable. Energy was fine. This phase felt easy.
Weeks 5-8: The Grind
72kg → 68kg
Training Days 2000 calories
Rest Days 1700 calories
Cardio 3x per week, 30min
Refeed Every 10 days
Progress slowed. This is where most people panic and slash calories harder. I stayed the course. Added one refeed day every 10 days to keep my sanity. Lost another 4kg.
Weeks 9-12: The Finish
68kg → 63kg
Training Days 1900 calories
Rest Days 1600 calories
Cardio 4-5x per week, 40-50min
Refeed Every 7 days
Hardest phase. I was lean, tired, and hungry most days. But I was close. Refeeds more frequent to keep metabolism going. Final 5kg came off. Worth every minute.
